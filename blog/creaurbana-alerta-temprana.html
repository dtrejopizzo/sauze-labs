When the Contract Works Against You

April 27, 2026 Maria Agustina Colloca

An early-warning model for contractual index mismatch in construction finance. Real estate industry — Argentina.

Developing infrastructure projects in emerging economies like Argentina means operating under a set of forces that no conventional model can integrate simultaneously: persistent and non-linear inflation, extreme currency volatility, regulatory frameworks that are reconfigured with every political cycle, heavy reliance on imported inputs subject to access restrictions, and trade unions with negotiating power that transcends market logic.

The Problem: The Curse of Static Assumptions

The central question is not how to optimize a contract — it is how to make rational financial decisions when the fundamental variables are inherently uncertain and politically sensitive. Risk management stops being a technical exercise and becomes a problem of strategic intelligence.

A client was facing exactly this: a 26-month construction contract where the adjustment indices on both the cost and revenue sides were continuously decoupling from the model used to structure the original bid. Every month, a new gap. Every quarter, uncertainty about whether to renegotiate or absorb the loss.

The Solution: Modeling Complexity

Artificial intelligence represents the qualitative leap that makes this approach viable at scale. Where a traditional statistical model works with means and standard deviations, the engine developed by Sauze Labs integrates complete macroeconomic and political scenarios: inflation, exchange rate dynamics, union wage adjustments, and construction progress curves are combined dynamically to project the behavior of the CAC and UOCRA indices month by month throughout the full life of the contract.

The result: a projected loss of $525M on a 26-month contract, with an actual verified loss of $521M — a deviation of less than 1%, using projected indices, not observed ones.

Impact on the Client

Direct savings identified

Quantifying the historical index mismatch made it possible to identify the loss amount attributable to the contract structure, opening the door to data-backed renegotiations with the counterparty.

Contract redesign

The client restructured the adjustment clauses in new contracts, incorporating hedging mechanisms against index divergence — something that had previously been entirely off the financial team's radar.

Commercial strategy reoriented

The analysis led to a rethink of the sales strategy and pricing approach for future projects, incorporating index mismatch risk as an explicit variable in the profitability equation.

The Sauze Differential: Political Intelligence

In Argentina and across Latin America, economic outcomes cannot be explained by economics alone. The moment a union negotiates, the government's posture toward labor demands, the political cycle, and the accumulated real-wage deficit are all variables that determine the timing and magnitude of adjustments — and therefore the real risk profile of a contract.

The model explicitly incorporates a layer of political sensitivity: the Union Recovery Factor, calibrated against the union's historical purchasing power trajectory, allows the user to adjust the projection based on their reading of the political-union context. Moderate when the government has capacity for containment. Aggressive when the union has accumulated years of losses and the political environment is permissive. This dimension transforms a financial model into a tool of strategic intelligence.

Architecture of Value: Four Layers Where AI Creates Real Impact

Layer 1 — Data

Automatic extraction and integration of historical CAC, UOCRA, and CPI indices. Direct reading of the client's contractual spreadsheet for calibration.

Layer 2 — Engine

Dynamic simulation: month-by-month projection using calibrated construction progress curves, union wage escalation steps, and parametric adjustment factors.

Layer 3 — Context

Political and union intelligence layer: Union Recovery Factor calibrated to the union's historical purchasing power. Sensitivity to the political timing of negotiations.

Layer 4 — Decision

Scenario interface: interactive comparison of A/B/C hypotheses. The executive team navigates scenarios in real time without technical intervention.

Scalability: Why AI Makes This Possible

Portfolio management across complex contracts: The engine is designed to analyze simultaneously entire portfolios of active contracts — different maturities, adjustment indices, and counterparties — identifying risk concentration and aggregate exposure in real time.

Continuous monitoring and early alerts: With integration into public data sources (INDEC, CAMARCO), the model recalibrates monthly and issues alerts when the index trajectory exceeds predefined risk thresholds by contract.

Applicable to any index pair: The architecture is sector-agnostic: it applies to any contract where revenues and costs adjust by different indices — construction, energy, concessions, regulated services.

Replicable across Latin America: The index mismatch problem is structural in high-inflation economies. The model is exportable to Chile, Colombia, Mexico, and Brazil with local calibration.
